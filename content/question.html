    Growth strategy for Carbonated beverage Co.

    • Welcome to the 2022 Bain case competition!

    • Your team has been invited by the Chief Strategy Officer of Carbonated beverage Co. to participate in an internal workshop for Carbonated beverage Co.’s Growth Strategy in China. The quality of your presentation will determine whether you will have a next round discussion with the leadership team (CEO, CFO, CSO, COO, etc. will attend) on a potential consulting project

    • Mainland China carbonated beverage market can be divided into cola carbonates, non-cola carbonates with sugar and non-cola carbonates without sugar

    • Carbonated beverage Co. is a top brand in non-cola carbonates segment

    • In recent years, the market is growing fast, and the players are expanding rapidly. Carbonated beverage Co. is facing with intense competition and uncertainty is hovering around the company’s future

    • In a pre-discussion with the Chief Strategy Officer, she mentioned several growth opportunities, incl. penetrating more channels / point-of- sales, entering adjacency segments, etc.

    • The Chief Strategy Officer asked for your team’s help in thinking through the following key questions and implications to Carbonated beverage Co. (assume no major disruption, e.g., COVID, financial downturn):

      • How would overall non-cola carbonates market evolve in the next 5 years and what are the key drivers?

      • What is the current competitive landscape of non-cola carbonates market, and what are some key trends within the next 5 years?

      • Who should be Carbonated beverage Co.’s primary target consumers? What are their profiles and key purchasing criteria? What are Carbonated beverage

        Co.’s value proposition among those key purchasing criteria?

      • What are the main distribution channels for non-cola carbonates (excl. the eCOM)? How does Carbonated beverage Co. perform amongst different channels

        compared with competitors? What’s the feedback from store owner and distributors?

    • Your team would need to tell a compelling story supported by robust data analysis to answer the above-mentioned 4 key questions

    • This proposal is due by May 5, 11:59pm, and you may be invited to present these materials in the workshop in the following week

    • To ensure that your presentation hits the most important points and is “communicating-for-results”, you have agreed with the Chief Strategy Officer that

      • Maximum 15 slides for the presentation (not including agenda pages or additional data analyses in backup materials) - the objective is not supposed to be as comprehensive as possible, so please focus on what is most insightful and impactful
      • Minimum 26 font size for slide taglines; minimum 14 font size for main body text; maximum 3 charts/graphs on a slide
      • All pages should be easy to read and understand for audience with a general background

    • To set a “Point of Departure”, your team can either choose an existing player like the prior description as the hypothetical client (e.g., Genki Forest, Nongfu Spring, Coca Cola, Heytea bottle etc.) or assume a nonexistent player with similar characteristics

    • To help you quickly get up-to-speed, the Chief Strategy Officer’s team has kindly shared some reference materials they have been working on for the company’s growth strategy. She believes that your team will be able to gather other interesting and powerful information from publicly available sources (e.g. company annual reports, analyst reports, business news) or primary research to support your analysis and recommendation

    • Again, the Chief Strategy Officer remind you to follow and focus on the 4 key questions, given the tight timeline
